FAQ

What is the standard currency on the island?

The official currency of Taniti is the US Dollar. Businesses often accept euros or yen as well as credit cards. Many banks facilite currency exchange.

When can I purchase alcohol?

Alcohol can be served from 9:00 AM until midnight daily.

Do Tanitians celebrate any special holidays?

Yes. Tanitians celebrate many national holidays. Many businesses will be closed so tourists should plan ahead carefully.

What is the legal drinking age?

The drinking age on Taniti is 18. This law is not strictly enforced.

Do I need to bring a power outlet adapter for my electronics?

No. Taniti power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the US.

What is the native language spoken in Taniti?

In Tanity City English is spoken often. Most younger people speak English fluently. In rural areas, older residents often do not speak English.

Do I have to worry about crime while in Taniti?

No. Violent crime is extremely low on Taniti. However, as tourism increases there has been more reports of pickpocketing and other minor crimes.

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.
